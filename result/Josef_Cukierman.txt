Josef Cukierman

Josef (Józef, Iosif) Cukierman (Zukermann) (1900, Poland – 1941, France) was a Polish-born French chess master.

Biography
Cukierman was born near Białystok, in 1900. He won the second Moscow City Championship (1920/21).[1] In early 1920s, Cukierman lived in Białystok, Poland, where he won a chess club championship in 1926. Then he emigrated to France.[2]
In 1928, he tied for 2nd-3rd with Leon Schwartzmann, behind Abraham Baratz, in the 4th Paris City Championship.[3] In 1929, he tied for 5-6th in Paris (Savielly Tartakower won). In 1930, he won, ahead of Tartakower, in the 6th Paris Championship. In 1931, he won in Paris. In 1933, he took 6th in Paris (Alexander Alekhine won). In 1938, Cukierman took 3rd, behind José Raúl Capablanca and Nicolas Rossolimo, in Paris. In 1939, he tied for 5-6th in Paris (Rossolimo won).[4]
During World War II, he committed suicide in 1941.
